# Search Intent Workflow

## Before Writing

Answer these in the draft brief:

1. Who is the buyer or operator?
2. What job are they trying to complete?
3. What pain or risk triggered the search?
4. What exact query would they type?
5. What adjacent fan-out queries might an AI/search engine use?
6. What region, language, compliance, or buying norm changes the answer?
7. What proof from `wiki/` or `raw/` makes this answer credible?
8. What action should the reader take next?

Start with the customer, not the keyword. A keyword is only useful when it maps to a real buyer/operator job, anxiety, constraint, or comparison.

## Length and Beauty

Content length is determined by intent:

- Troubleshooting: concise answer first, then steps. Usually 800-1400 words.
- Setup guide: steps, screenshots, pitfalls. Usually 1200-2200 words.
- Product page: strong value, use cases, proof, FAQ. Usually 700-1400 words.
- Comparison: criteria table, tradeoffs, when to choose each. Usually 1400-2500 words.
- Landing page: shorter copy, stronger sections, visual hierarchy.

Avoid walls of text. Use:

- Short paragraphs.
- Tables for comparisons.
- Bullets for checklists.
- Screenshots near the relevant step.
- A summary block for GEO and featured answer potential.

## De-AI Review

Flag and rewrite:

- "In today's fast-paced digital landscape"
- "seamless", "robust", "cutting-edge" without proof
- repeated sentence rhythm
- vague benefits without operational detail
- generic conclusions
- excessive transitions
- unsupported superlatives

Replace with:

- specific buyer situations
- concrete workflows
- product limits and tradeoffs
- screenshots or examples
- direct next steps
